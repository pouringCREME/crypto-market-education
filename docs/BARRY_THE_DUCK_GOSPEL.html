Part II

Understanding Barry

The Idiot-Proof Field Guide

What Barry Actually Does (No Jargon)

Think of a river system.

Most trading tools look at the surface — today's price, today's volume, the last candle. They see the ripple and call it a wave.

Barry looks at the whole watershed:

  • Where is the water coming from? (on-chain flows, macro regimes)
  • How fast is it moving at each level? (multi-scale momentum)
  • What's happening three rivers upstream that hasn't hit the surface yet?

Then he tells you what he sees. You decide whether to fish.

The 4 Scales — Barry's Four Gospels

Every signal Barry surfaces has an origin scale. This is the single most important thing to understand when reading Barry's output.

ScaleWhat It IsTime HorizonThink of It As
MicroIndividual price ticks, short candlesMinutes to hoursThe ripple on the surface
MesoMoving averages, short-term momentumHours to daysThe current in the river
MacroStructural trends, regime shiftsDays to weeksThe flow of the whole river
Structural / On-chainBridge flows, wallet activity, cross-chain dataWeeks to monthsThe source of the river upstream

Key rule: The higher the origin scale, the more significant the signal.

  • Micro-origin signal at 54% → interesting but noisy. Watch it.
  • Macro-origin signal at 65% → Barry is seeing upstream movement. Pay attention.
  • Structural/on-chain signal at any confidence → something is moving before the market knows it.

Barry will tell you when he's below threshold. He doesn't hide it. If he flags noise, believe him.

Signal Origin (the most important metric)

When Barry tells you a signal came from macro or cross_chain_bridge_surge — that's the source of the pattern, not the price action you can see on a chart.

Checklist when reading a Barry signal:

  • What scale did it originate from? (higher = more significant)
  • What is the confidence %?
  • Did Barry flag any uncertainty or noise?
  • What confirmation event did he say to watch for next?
  • Has the next scale down confirmed yet?

If macro said LONG and meso hasn't confirmed yet — the signal is early. That's not bad. That's the point. Barry is upstream.

Fitness Score (Evolution demos)

When Barry runs his evolutionary population, the fitness score tells you how well a strategy genome is performing against historical patterns.

  • 1.0 = baseline
  • 1.47 = meaningfully better than baseline
  • Watch which genomes survive across generations. Persistence = robustness.

Curiosity Score (Character State)

Barry has an internal temperament that shifts with data quality.

High curiosity during a signal = Barry found something in difficult conditions. Weight it appropriately.

Walls Hit

The count of data gaps, missing feeds, or dead ends Barry encountered during a run.

  • 0 walls = clean data, clean signal
  • Walls present but signal still surfaced = Barry adapted. Note the resilience.
  • Many walls + low confidence = treat output as exploratory, not actionable.
